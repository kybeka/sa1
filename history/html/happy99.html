    Happy99 Virus

    Surely one of the most famous cases in recent viruses history is a non-malignant malware that wasn’t created with the intent of destroying or damaging files, but it can be considered as the proof of concept of self-propagating viruses. This worm was created in the early days of 1999 by an anonymous French developer known only by his nickname “Spanska” which was used to describe his creation as a “sympathetic hitchhiker who uses your internet connection to travel, and thank you for the trip with a small animation”.

    Talking about the technical aspect Happy99 is a worm that arrives in inbox as an attachment or more simply as an email. When the user will open the Happy99.exe files the worm will be executed showing a window of fireworks and wishes for the new year 2000. At this point the worm will modify different file in order to send itself to any email in the user’s mail server.

    As said the content of this worm is completely harmless and apparently without any significant meaning, but it’s important to remember that this is the first virus that was able to automatically propagate itself by email. For this reason from the Happy99’s model will be created the next generation of self-propagating viruses.
