    The Pegasus Spyware

    What is it?

    Pegasus is a spyware created by the Isreaeli technology firm NSO Group. Pegasus is able to get access to devices running on Android, iOS and Blackberry. In its earlier versions, before any security updates came, the Spyware basically turned any infected smarthphone into a powerful surveillance device, thanks to its ability to access or read text messages, passwords, tracking calls and even going as far as accessing the microphone and the camera.

    The founding company, assures that it only provided authorized governments with technology that helps them combat terror and crime[1]. But many believe that the spyware was used with malicious intent as well. An example is the death of journalist Jamal Khashoggi who was assassinated for not sharing the same view of its government by agents of Saudi Arabia, which allegedly used Pegasus to monitor Khashoggi's smarthphone and position[2].

    How does it work?

    The earliest versions of Pegasus were installed by spear-pishing[3], by tricking the target into clicking a web-link or to open a document containing the Spyware. Nowadays though, Pegasus has evolved so much that the software can be installed through missed whatsapp calls or by sending a message, both of which leave zero trace on a device, meaning that the user will not have any idea that malicious software has been installed.

    Pegasus' first intentions were certainly good, its initial objective was to only target criminals. Nowadays, with many governments having the ability to use the Spyware, who get's to decide if their use is correct or not? Who can objectively decide whether a person is a threat to a country or only to a government's image?
