1982 - The Elk Cloner

The First Virus

While the Creeper might have been the first computer virus, the first worm to actually spread outside of the system in which it was designed to function was the Elk Cloner, which was created by Rich Skrenta in 1982, when he was only 15 years old. Like the Creeper, the intention behind the Elk cloner was not to cause damage to the computer, however, unlike the Creeper, it also was not created as an experiment, but rather as a prank. To this day there is still an ongoing debate in regards to which of the two programs was the first real virus.

Rich Skrenta

Rich learnt how to program in Basic and in assembly language when he recieved an Apple II computer for his 13th birthday. At the time, it was popular to share pirated computer games through floppy disks, something also Rich did with his friends.

He decided to take advantage of this to mess with his friends and started modifying the disk so that after a program had been run a few times, the next time it was opened it would either shut down the computer or display a message. However, his friends eventually stopped trusting him so, in order to keep pranking them, he had to develop a program that would stay in their computers' memories and copy itself to other floppy disks independently, something he accomplished to do in the span of two weeks.

Like Ray Tomlinson, Rich eventually also wrote a program to erase the Elk cloner from infected computers, since he didn't want the program to become too widespread, as that was not the intention of the virus.
