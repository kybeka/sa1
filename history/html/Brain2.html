    1986 - Brain Computer Virus

    How Brain works

    Brain, also known as Pakistani Flu, was a BOOT sector virus that was loaded into the computer without the user's knowledge when the computer was turned on from an infected floppy disk. The ability of Brain to load itself into the computer before the operating system made it unique. By replacing the boot section of the floppy disk with a copy of the virus, Brain infects the IBM computer. The boot sector is moved to a different location and designated as corrupted.

    When the Brain system tries to read the infected boot sector, it just displays the original boot sector. When the virus is operating in memory, everything appears normal when looking at the boot sector using DEBUG or a similar tool. As a consequence, this virus is also known as the first "stealth" virus.

    Brain's end

    When the brothers began receiving a huge number of phone calls from people in the United Kingdom, the United States, and other countries requesting that they disinfect their machines, they were taken aback and tried to explain to the irate callers that their motivation was not malicious. Their phone lines had been overburdened. The brothers continued their operation in Pakistan as Brain NET Internet service providers through a company called Brain Telecommunication Limited, together with another brother, Shahid Farooq Alvi.
