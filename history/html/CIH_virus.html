    CIH Virus
    Chernobyl Spacefiller

    The CIH virus (alternatively known as the Chernobyl or Spacefiller virus) was a computer virus which emerged in 1998. and believed to have been created by Chen Ing-hau, a student at Tatung University in Taiwan. The virus creates a highly destructive payload on vulnerable systems and overwriting critical information on infected system drives, and in certain cases even destroying the system BIOS. It is assumed that around 60 million computers were affected internationally, resulting in $1 billion dollars worth of commercial damages.

    Chen had claimed he had written the virus in a bold attempt to challend the antiviral efficiency by antivirus software developers. In an unexpected turn of events, the virus had spread across students at Tatung University by Chen's classmates. In an attempt to apologize, Chen made an antivirus program publicly available for download (which was made in cooperation with another student - Weng Shi-hao - at Tamkang University). Chen was never able to be publicly prosecuted because no victims ever cam forward with a lwasuit, however, this event inspired new "computer crime" legislation in Taiwan.

    The name "Chernobyl Virus" was coined some time after the virus was already well known as CIH, and refers to the complete coincidence of the payload trigger date in some variants of the virus (actually the virus creation date in 1998, to trigger exactly a year later) and the Chernobyl disaster, which happened in the Soviet Union on April 26, 1986.

    In March of 1999, several thousand IBM Aptivas were believed to have been shipped with the CIH virus, just one full month before the virus would officially trigger. In July of 1999, at one of the worlds most notable hacker conventions - DEF CON 7 - attendees were discovered by organizers to have been "infected" by the virus. On December 31st, of the same year Yamaha had released a software update to their drives that had been infected with the virus. A year earlier, a first-person shooter game SiN was infected by one of its mirror sites.

    CIH's dual payload was executed worldwide for the first time April 26, 1999 and affected countries in Asia the most. It works by filling the first 1024 KB of the host's boot drive with zeros and then moves on to attack the BIOS. The payloads serve to render the host computer inoperable, and for "ordinary" users the virus practically destroys the hard disk data.

    The virus is not as common nowadays as it was in the 2000's, but it did make a comeback in 2001 when a variant of the LoveLetter Worm in a VBS file that contained a dropper routine for the CIH virus - circulating under the pretense of containing nude photos of Jennifer Lopez.
