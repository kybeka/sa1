    2003 - SQL Slammer

    How was it born?

    In June 2002, Microsoft realesed a patch that fixed the bug that the worm exploited six months later: a buffer overflow bug affecting Microsoft's database based on Desktop Engine and SQL Server products. Hoverwer many people didn't find the patch necessary, leaving their system vulnerable.

    In January 2003, the worm was released. It has already attacked and infected 75'000 internet servers within 10 minutes of its release. With 13'000 ATMs no longer working for customers, Bank of America is one of the largest firms afflicted by the infection.

    The damage worsened when the worm caused a global Internet slowdown on January 25. It has been reported that the worm interfered with calls to the 911 emergency number and was responsable for many canceled flights.

    How does it work?

    The worm was noticed in early January 25, 2003, when it was slowing down system around the world. The slowdown was due to the breakdown of many routers under the weight of extremely high traffic from infected servers. Typically, when the traffic is too high for the routers, this one should delay or temporarily stop network traffic. Instead, some routers have crashed that have been stopped and should not be contacted. Routers began sending notifications to the other known routers for this purpose. The deluge of routing table update notifications caused some additional routers to fail, making the problem worse. Soon, routers communicating with each other to update routing tables were consuming a significant amount of Internet badnwidth, and regular traffic slowerd or, in some cases, stopped altogheter.
