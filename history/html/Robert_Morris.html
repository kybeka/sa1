    Robert Tappan Morris

    The Morris Worm

    In November 2, 1988, the Morris worm was the first ever computer worms distributed via Internet and also the first that have gain a mainstream media attention. The creator of this virus, Robert Morris,was the first person who was persecuted for creating a computer virus becasue has violated the laws on Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. The worm was distributed by the computer systems of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Robert Morris created the worm simply to see if it could be done.

    Robert Tappan Morris

    Robert Tappan Morris is a computer scientist and enterpreneur. He is best known for the developing of the Morris worm, the first ever computer worm on Internet. He was prosecuterd for the developing and releasing of the worm, and became the first person convicted under the new Computer Fraud and Abuse Act Law. He is also the cofounder of Viaweb , one of the first web applications, with Paul Graham. The two also funding Y Combinator

    Robert Morris began his career as a computer scientist at Bell Labs, where he assisted in the development of Multics and Unix, and later rose to become the top scientist of the National Computer Security Center, a part of the National Security Agency.

    Morris attended Harvard University, and latterly went on to graduate academy at Cornell University. In the first year of school, he created a computer worm that destroyed several machines on the internet, which was also in its infancy at the time.

    Criminal prosecution

    In 1989, Robert Morris, was indicted for violating the article about the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. He became the first ever person that was prosecuted for this act. In December 1990, came the court ruling that condemned him to 3 years of probation, 400 hours of community service and a fine of $10'050 plus the cost of his supervision.

    Complicating matters because Robert Morris was the son of a respected scientist of Bell Labs and US National Agency.

    But Morris story is still not end, in fact, went on to become a renowned academic, tech founder and venture capitalist and still today is a professor of computer science and artificial intelligence at MIT's Electrical Engineering & Computer Science department .
