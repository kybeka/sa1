    Backup Plan

    With the increase in number of cyberattacks, data loss is becoming more and more frequent at the level of individuals as well as at the level of companies. No one is or was truly safe from data loss, hence the need for a robust backup strategy that permits a quick recovery from lost data. In this article, we will take a look at some of the possible strategies and principles to follow.





    At the Individual Level

    To protect data at an individual level, it is possible to copy data to a hardware such as an external hard drive, a CD, or to a USB drive, the other option is to use a cloud backup service (the data is stored in a data center and is accessible online, it usually comes with a versioning system). Having multiple copies of the data ensures that the data is always available even if one of the storing systems fails. Furthermore a backupsoftware can be used to make the task of managing the backup easier.

    At the Company Level

    Making sure that data is not lost at the Level of companies is a far more complex and challenging task, particularly because of the size of the data to be stored and the task of managing it.

    Classifying Data

    Because storing and maintaining huge amounts of data is costly and difficult, the company has to make a choice about which part of the data should be stored and how frequently should it be updated. The priority is given to critical data without which the company can no longer continue to exist.

    Recovery Time

    Another important aspect to take into consideration is the recovery time, in other words, the required time to get the data back from the storage into the working environment. A company would have a limited amount of time to recover important data without causing much harm to its reputation or resources, and depending on this amount of time, the company might consider a certain choice or strategy concerning the backing up of data.

    Storage Medium

    When it comes to how and where the data should be stored, the company has the choice to implement the cloud backup services of a third party, this solution is particularly useful in case the company wants to secure its data without hiring any additional manpower or placing pressure on its IT staff but the price and the recovery time of doing so increases for a big amount of data. The other possibility is storing the data on a disk owned by the company, this choice makes for a faster recovery time but is expensive, storing the data offline ensures the safety of the stored data from malware infection,nevertheless, this comes at the price of accessibility. The company might as well decide to encrypt its data to secure it from unauthorized access but this also has its drawbacks in terms of price, accessibility, and logistics. Thus the company must decide which type of data to be stored in what way and where.
