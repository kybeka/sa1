    Common Sense

    Common sense is definitely another valid system to use to protect yourself.

    First of all, stronger password should be something ordinary to do in order to prevent yourself from being a victim of theft.
    Some of the most familiar methods used by hackers to discover user’s passwords are the following:

    • The dictionary attack: the use of a program that combines dictionary’s words in the most common ways.
    • Phishing: the scammers find a way, usually by pretending to be a trusted entity in digital communication, to make you give them valuable information (such as passcodes or money) through calls, emails, etc.
    • Brute-force search (or exhaustive search): done with the use of an automated program that combines characters until it finds the password.

    The longer and the more passwords contain different characters, the more difficult it will be for hackers to steal it.






    Another technique is malware, informatics’ program that infect your computer or smartphone to spy and to steal information. For protect yourself from this risk you should pay attention to attached links that arrive in emails or that open up when you visit a web page. Also, very important is to be careful when you download something from an unreliable website.

    Another key point, that most of the time it is a matter underestimated by many people, is giving close and thoughtful attention to being connected to a secure network, topic that will be discussed later on.
