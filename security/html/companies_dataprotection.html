    CYBER PROTECTION FOR COMPANIES

    Companies and businesses are often subject to cyber attacks therefore they require a system that allows them to face these threats without having to shut down the entire firm. Nowadays managers rely on two specific plans which are called business continuity and disaster recovery plan.






    RECOVERY PLANS

    Business continuity plan includes procedures and instructions that a business should follow when a disaster happens so that the business can continue to operate. Disaster recovery plans on the other hand ensures that business processes are restored within a certain amount of time called the recovery time and continue normal operations. Disasters can range from hardware failure to fire flood or even massive cyber attacks. The primary purpose of the disaster recovery plan is to get technical operations back to normal in the shortest time possible so it allows the business to recover from the disaster. As an example we can suppose a hurricane has affected the normal business operations of a business. The business continuity plan will ensure that the critical business operations remain functional during and immediately after the hurricane; the employees can start working from home and ensure that the critical business operations are functional. But this cannot be a long term solution. The disaster recovery plan w ill ensure that damaged pieces of equipment are replaced and employees can return to the office location and continue working. The disaster recovery plan will also ensure that data and critical applications are restored from backup so that the business can continue regular operations.

    BACK UPs

    When it comes to essential data for the company we don't want to backup just for the sake of backing up, we want to back up and have meaningful backups that we can use for business. We want to make sure that we have backups that cover day to day work, we want to have backups to cover potential loss when the system is down and we want to have the data in a location where the recovery is accessible even in a disaster event. In this field there are essentially three types of backup: real-time backups, periodic backups and geolocation data back up. In real-time backup the data is stored as close to real time, is backed up on adrive or can be a mirrored system. This data must be available for retrieval in at least 15 minutes so that the system can be taken down for a data transfer and brought up. Periodic backup allows to go back and select a period of time where we can restore the data and know exactly what data to put in subsequent to it. Geolocation backups refers to data that is located in a different geographic region, usually different for kind of risks.
