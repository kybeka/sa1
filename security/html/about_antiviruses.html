    About antiviruses




    What is an antivirus?


    Antivirus software is used to protect computers from viruses by scanning, detecting, and removing them. Most antivirus software operates in the background once installed, providing real-time protection against virus attacks. Comprehensive virus protection systems guard your files and hardware against malware like worms, Trojan horses, and spyware, and may also include features like customized firewalls and website blocking.

    What is Malware?


    Malware, short for "malicious software," is a catch-all phrase for a wide range of software applications meant to cause harm or perform other undesirable acts on a computer, server, or network. Viruses, malware, and trojan horses are common examples. Malware can cause your device to slow down, malfunction, or erase files. Malware is frequently used by criminals to deliver spam, collect personal and financial information, and even steal identities.

    What is phishing?


    Phishing attacks employ email or fraudulent websites to try to deceive you into supplying personal or financial information in order to compromise an account or steal money. They may say that there is an issue with your payment information or that they've spotted activity on your account, and they'll ask you to click on a link or open a file and supply personal information.
