    VPN

    VPN, or virtual private network, thanks to the tunneling system (Wiki) it makes invisible the activities on the network to unauthorized third parties and the IP address from which you access. This is made possible via the Internet and not by a set of cables or a physical system. In a nutshell, VPN allows you to surf the web by hiding your IP and allowing you to connect with another one. In this way it allows a secure connection and when you connect to a network it encrypts your connection preventing the recognition of your identity. Always thanks to VPN, which encrypts the connection, it is therefore possible to access sites that are banned or restricted in your country.

    VPN is basically of 2 types:

    1. remote access VPN: it allows a user to connect and to access a network remotely. Useful for both home and business users. The connection is over the internet and is secure and private.
    2. Site to site VPN: it’s commonly used in large companies, with branches in different locations, so as to connect the network of one location to that of another

    The site-to-site VPN creates a communication “pathway” between networks in distant offices and connects them via the internet supporting secure and private communication. This type of VPN is divided into two categories: Intranet based VPN: when different offices of a company are connected via site-to-site vpn. Extranet based VPN: when companies use site to site VPN to connect to another company's office.







    Why use VPN?

    Let explain it with an example, i.e. think of a store with branches in different countries that need to communicate with each other for accounting purpose etc. Unfortunately, sending such information without precautions is not safe: from the modem where you send the documents to the service provider or even to other companies, at any point you may be in danger from a cybercriminal. That is why it’s better to use a VPN that encrypts the content of your message.


    What risks are there when using a VPN?

    Despite the fact that VPN applications guarantee security and privacy we can be confronted with a lot of risks, this could happen for example with free VPN services. When we agree to connect to a VPN, we allow it to control the connection on our behalf. The message is encrypted until it reaches the server, while there are no security measures afterwards. Many free VPN providers record your internet traffic and use this information for commercial or intelligence purposes. Moreover, the VPN server you are connected to is operated in accordance with laws of that country. Keep in mind that VPN services only encrypt internet traffic, but there are many dangers on the internet: viruses, dangerous web pages and etc., they can all infect your computer with a file downloaded with the VPN service or through a visited web page.
