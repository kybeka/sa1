    Software Management

    Before anything else, it’s necessary that we talk about the importance of downloading updates: doing this simple action will decrease the risk of being affected by malware and viruses. With the progression of cell phones this problem does not only include computers but also smartphones.

    Matter of fact, in today’s world any device connected to the Internet can be hacked.







    What does this hackability consist of?

    Hackers take advantages of the vulnerabilities in operating system or application. By updating these as each update arrives, flaws are fixed before they are targeted. No operating system or application is perfect, and in fact updates are often released, slowing down the work of hackers and throwing all their work into the trash while also protecting your personal data, such as password, bank access and so on. In addition, updates can have new features or remove the outdated ones, making the compatibility with other devices better and often they include software patches, which cover the security holes to keep hackers out.


    Another factor of great importance for keeping your devices updated, although not much considered, is that the cyber security is not just all about you: if you have a virus on your device, the chances that you could spread it to your friends and business associates is very high. The way you can get your devices infected by a virus are many and they could be such as just viewing a rogue website, reading a damaged message or viewing media that are infected. Once the virus is insinuated on your device, the malware could steal your data or even give to the attacker the control over your device to encrypt your files.







    Why is it so important to update your software?

    An example that demonstrates well the importance of always updating your system is the WannaCry attack that exploited the EternalBlue vulnerability in Microsoft systems: briefly, EternalBlue is an exploit, written by the NSA (National security Agency), that was stolen and illegally spread in April 2017 by a hacker group called The Shadow Brokers. This diffusion was later used to realize the cyber-attack in May 2017 via the ransomware WannaCry, which when it's running it encrypts the files on your computer and demands a ransom to decrypt them. This EternalBlue takes advantage of a vulnerability in the SMB (Server Message Block) protocol present in Microsoft operating systems, now listed CVE-2017-0144 in the Common Vulnerabilities and Exposure catalog. Microsoft had prepared software patches to fix this issue, when it was alerted by the NSA of the possible theft of the eternal blue, that were released in March 2017. Unfortunately, as is often the case, many users did not install the aforementioned patch (called MS17-010) and two months after its release the WannaCry ransomware attack happened. Immediately the day after the attack Microsoft released emergency security patches. Despite this, at the end of 2018 many systems were still vulnerable to EternalBlue, as these updates were not executed, which led to a large number of damages.
