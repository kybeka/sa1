    TROJAN

    A Trojan is harmful code or software that appears to be legitimate yet has the ability to take control of your machine. A Trojan is a computer program that is designed to hurt, disrupt, steal, or otherwise harm your data or network.

    The malware included within is activated when the user installs and executes the malicious program onto a device. Once the Trojan software has been downloaded and installed, cyber thieves can seize control of the device and employ ransomware to lock the user out.

    The majority of Trojan files are designed to look like they came as an email attachment from a known email address. The malicious content inside can spread worms throughout a network, disrupting performance and posing a data security risk. As individuals navigate the web, the files can seem as work files, image attachments, or even links to websites.





    HOW TROJANS WORK?

    How the Trojan horse infects a computer depends on its design.
    The primary goal of a Trojan horse as it infects a computer is to:

    • Delete data on the device
    • Copies of data are made to be stolen and sold, or to be used for other nefarious purposes.
    • Modify data
    • Block data or access to data
    • Disrupt the performance of the target computer and/or network






    MOST COMMON TROJANS INFECTION POINTS

    Email is one of the most common entry points for Trojan horses, malware, and other harmful stuff.
    When the source email address is reliable, many users don't hesitate to download email attachments. Hackers take advantage of this by enclosing malicious code and programs within the attachment. However, email isn't the only way for Trojans to get into your system.

    Most Internet security suites will alert users if a website has been compromised. It is not encouraged to visit a website with a bad security certificate or to proceed after a warning since this site is suspected of or known to be the source of Trojan horses. Trojans can also be spread through pop-up windows and suggested download links on these types of websites.







    HOW TROJANS IMPACT MOBILE DEVICES

    Trojans aren't just a concern for laptops and desktops. They can also have an influence on mobile devices, such as phones and tablets. Cybercriminals frequently distribute them to unsuspecting consumers through unapproved and pirate app shops. These apps can steal information from smartphones and make income by sending premium SMS texts after they've been installed.

    SWITCHER TROJAN - 2015

    Some Android users were infected with a banking virus that could capture text messages containing financial information and send a copy to hackers through email, giving them all the information they needed to steal money.
